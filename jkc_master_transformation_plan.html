AI integration — Gemini + Brevo pipeline
1. Visitor intent classification — Gemini reads the "Questions / Prayer Request" field and classifies into: first-time visitor, returning seeker, language class interest, urgent pastoral need, general inquiry.

2. Pastoral email drafting — Gemini auto-drafts a personalised follow-up email from Pastor Marcel's voice. Appears in Mission Control as a one-click approve-and-send card. Pastor never starts from blank.

3. Brevo Welcome Journey — 5-email automated series triggered on form submit: (1) Welcome + next service info, (2) JKC intro video, (3) Kingdom Class invite, (4) Testimonies, (5) 30-day check-in. Bilingual EN/JP based on form language selection.

4. Twilio/WhatsApp alert — If Gemini detects urgency keywords in the prayer request, Pastor receives an immediate WhatsApp notification with visitor name and request summary.

Phase B · QR deep links + digital bulletin
Image 1 — replace dead seat QR codes with tracked, dynamic digital bulletin
Problem (from the seat cards)
Current QR codes point to www.mypurposejapan.com/connect — a dead or unrelated URL. Visitors scan and land nowhere useful. No analytics on who scanned, when, or what they did next.
New route: /connect
A "digital bulletin" page that shows: today's sermon title + notes link, visitor form CTA, next event, Kingdom Class info, and a "I'm new here" pathway. Changes weekly based on service date.
QR tracking
Each seat QR contains a unique ?src=seat-A3 param. Every scan logs to qr_scan_events table with timestamp, seat, device type, and whether the visitor completed the form.
Multi-surface QR
Beyond chairs: projection screen (before service), physical brochures, Easter event flyer, Kingdom Class schedule. Each surface gets its own QR code and src param for attribution.
Dynamic content per scan time
The /connect page detects the day of week and time. Sunday 10:00–12:00 = service mode (sermon notes + live stream link). Any other time = general visitor mode (about JKC, upcoming events, class registration). This makes the QR permanently relevant, not just useful on Sundays.

Phase E · Sermon notes — digital fill-in + live sync
Image 6 — bilingual notes (EN + JP) must be uploadable by admin and usable by members during and after service
What we're building from scratch
No table, no component exists for sermon notes. This is the most complex Phase E item because it spans three surfaces: Mission Control (admin upload), Watch page (live service), and Member profile (personal saved notes). It must work for both online and in-person attendees.
Mission Control (admin)
· Upload sermon notes template (PDF or structured outline)
· Input fill-in-the-blank fields with labels
· Set service date and attach to event
· Publish to Watch page
· After service: publish completed version
Watch page (during service)
· Notes appear alongside live stream
· Members type answers into fill-in fields
· Auto-save every 30 seconds to Supabase
· Guest mode: download blank PDF to print
· Language toggle: EN / 日本語
Member profile (post-service)
· Access all saved notes from past services
· Download personal notes as PDF
· Download completed "answer key" version
· Pastor can update notes any time
· Notes searchable by keyword or date
AI — post-service notes completion
After service, pastor or admin clicks "AI complete notes." Gemini is given:
· The sermon outline/title
· The scripture references (already in the notes)
· Any sermon recording transcript (if available)

Output: fill-in-the-blank answers generated automatically. Admin reviews, edits if needed, publishes to all members. Members who missed service still get the full experience. This is the most high-value single AI feature in the entire system.
New DB tableKey columns
sermon_notesid, service_date, title_en, title_jp, outline_json, scripture_refs, is_published, completed_json, org_id
member_sermon_notesid, profile_id, sermon_note_id, answers_json, last_saved_at, downloaded_at
Static export note: The auto-save feature requires Supabase Realtime or periodic REST calls — both work with static export. PDF generation should use a Supabase Edge Function with a headless Chrome/puppeteer layer or a service like PDFShift. Do not attempt PDF generation client-side.
